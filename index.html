Grapevine is a place for

Education

Grapevine allows users to view, sort, search, and share all current legislation in the state of Ohio. Users will be able to see what legislation is trending and read information about individual legislation.

Discussion

Users will be able to post and comment on a main page. Grapevine will strive to promote thoughtful and productive discussion on all things related to politics.

Data

Grapevine helps create powerful data to be utilized by citizens. Users can vote on legislation and take polls. It is important to note that these votes on Grapevine do not count for anything official. However, since Grapevine is only for registered voters, the votes on Grapevine should be a good representation of how voters feel.

Decisions

The data from Grapevine will empower citizens to be more informed and engaged when it’s time to make decisions at the voting booth. For example, before an election, the public will have access to representative’s voting record compared to the people from their district.

About Us

When it comes to government and politics, how many times have we heard somebody say something like, “They don’t represent us” or “The system is broken.” We talk about how there are problems, yet the problems persist. We need action. What are the problems? A lack of informed and engaged citizens. No data to monitor how well we are represented.

Today’s world of politics can be a turnoff for many reasons. One reason is the volume of legislation. Each state in the U.S. introduces hundreds of proposed bills and resolutions every year. At the national level Congress introduces thousands of bills every year. Most of us only hear about a small fraction of the legislation around us. We also have a lack of engagement in politics. Voter turnout for presidential elections is under 60% and the turnout is even lower for local elections.

In addition to not knowing enough about legislation and not fulfilling our duty to vote, we don’t have data to use to keep our representatives accountable. We can call or send mail to a representative, but where are the statistics on the number of people who were for or against an issue? The primary job of representatives is to represent us. Shouldn’t we have numbers and data to look at and monitor how well we are being represented?

What’s the solution? Grapevine. Education, discussion, data, decisions.

Grapevine is here to help in the world of politics in Ohio.
